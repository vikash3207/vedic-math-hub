## 1. What is a Base? (Explained for Beginners)

In Vedic Maths, a **base** is simply a **round number** that is easy to work with mentally.

### Common Bases Used
- Base **10** ‚Üí for small numbers
- Base **100** ‚Üí for 2‚Äëdigit numbers
- Base **1000** ‚Üí for 3‚Äëdigit numbers
- Base **10,000** ‚Üí for large numbers

### Why Use a Base?
Because our brain finds it **easier to calculate with round numbers** like 10, 100, or 1000.

Instead of calculating the full number, we calculate:
> **How far the number is from the base**

This distance is called the **complement**.

## 2. What is a Complement?

### Simple Definition
A **complement** tells us **how much we need to add to a number to reach the base**.

### Formula
Complement = Base ‚àí Number

## 4. Complements from Base 100

### Beginner-Friendly Rule
- Look at the **last two digits**
- Subtract them from **100**

### Examples
| Number | Complement | Explanation |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| 34 | 66 | 34 + 66 = 100 |
| 58 | 42 | 58 + 42 = 100 |
| 91 | 9 | 91 + 9 = 100 |
| 99 | 1 | 99 + 1 = 100 |

üëâ Do NOT change the number of digits mentally ‚Äî just think ‚Äúhow much to reach 100‚Äù.

## 5. Complements from Base 1000

## 6. Universal Complement Trick (Works for Any Base)

This is a **very powerful Vedic Maths trick**.

### Steps
1. Subtract **each digit from 9**, except the last digit
2. Subtract the **last digit from 10**

### Example: Complement of 462 (Base 1000)
- 4 ‚Üí 9 ‚àí 4 = 5
- 6 ‚Üí 9 ‚àí 6 = 3
- 2 ‚Üí 10 ‚àí 2 = 8

‚úÖ Complement = **538**

Check:  
462 + 538 = 1000 ‚úî

## 7. Why Complements Are So Important

Complements help you:
- Do **subtraction without borrowing**
- Multiply by **9, 99, 999**
- Multiply numbers **near 10, 100, 1000**
- Square numbers quickly
- Divide faster using Vedic methods

> Nearly every powerful Vedic Maths shortcut starts with complements.

## 8. Simple Daily-Life Example

### Example: 100 ‚àí 47
Instead of traditional subtraction:

- So, 100 ‚àí 47 = **53**

This saves time and avoids mistakes.
